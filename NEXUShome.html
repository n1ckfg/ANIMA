NEXUS - Network Projects by Artists

Investigation of aesthetic and critical issues on the interface between art and technology...

NEXUS supports exploratory projects by artists, theorists, and researchers in avante-garde and leading edge developments in the use of telecommunications as both content and medium of creativity. These projects vary in scope and intent, from the literary and critical to the expressive and visual.

Making Art Online

by Judy Malloy
MAKING ART ONLINE is a database of artists' words about using data transmission systems. Statements conveying information and ideas about making art online are interlinked by subject. The work is collaboratively written by artists using their own words to describe their projects.

Towards a Necessarily Furious Philosophy of the Discrete

by Oliver Hockenhull
A hopscotch of critical theory, new media research, and the interrogation of the aesthetics of the digital inscription played upon the canvas of language, power, and historical/political recollection

Imag(in)ing Networks

Hank Bull and the Western Front organised this telematic art event to celebrate art's Birthday, January 19th, 1994 by creating an interactive model of the network in the collective imagination. 

		How do you imagine the network?

            Is it a spiral, a nerve, a mathematical phrase
            molecular, monocular, rhythmic or knot
            tree form or free form or
            what have you got?
