"While it was the doubling of brain size that accounted for the 
speciation of Homo Habilis into Homo Erectus, it was the doubling of 
brain function, ie. the radicalization of deduplication of cerebral 
operations that accounted for the emergence and elaboration of verbal 
speech, and the speciation of Homo Erectus into Homo Sapiens.  
The rudiments of cerebral dominance, the laterilized asymmetry of 
hemispheric function, required radical, dialectic type differentation 
before syntax, semantics and speech could be.  The physical, social and 
protocultural environment requirments imposed on evolving human gene pools 
provoked the necessary speciations that granted survival. "

"Explicit operations are more subject to hemispheric independence 
(cerebral segragation) , while implicit operations involve a greater 
measure of hemispheric interdependence. (cerebral integration)."
